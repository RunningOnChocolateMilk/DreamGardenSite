September Gardening Guide: Spring Planting in Australia

Published January 15, 2025 • 8 min read

Spring is here! Discover what to plant in September, essential garden preparation tips, and how DreamGarden will help you create a thriving spring garden in Australia's unique climate.

September marks the beginning of spring across most of Australia, and it's one of the most exciting times for gardeners. The days are getting longer, temperatures are rising, and the soil is warming up – perfect conditions for planting and growing. Whether you're a seasoned gardener or just starting out, this guide will help you make the most of September's gardening opportunities.

Why September is Perfect for Planting

September offers ideal conditions for successful gardening across Australia:

  • Optimal Temperatures: Soil temperatures are warming up but not yet too hot for young plants
  • Increased Daylight: Longer days provide more sunlight for photosynthesis and growth
  • Rainfall Patterns: Spring rains help establish new plants without the intensity of summer storms
  • Growing Season Ahead: Maximum time for plants to establish before winter returns
  • Pest Control: Many garden pests are less active in early spring

What to Plant in September

September is prime time for planting a wide variety of vegetables, herbs, and flowers. Here's your comprehensive planting guide:

🌱 Vegetables to Plant Now

Warm Season

Tomatoes

Plant seedlings now for summer harvest. Choose varieties suited to your climate zone.

Warm Season

Capsicums & Peppers

Plant seedlings in warm, sunny positions. Great for containers and garden beds.

Warm Season

Eggplants

Plant in rich, well-draining soil. Needs consistent warmth and regular feeding.

Cool Season

Lettuce

Plant seeds or seedlings for continuous harvest. Perfect for succession planting.

Cool Season

Spinach

Plant seeds in partial shade. Harvest leaves as needed for continuous supply.

Cool Season

Carrots

Sow seeds directly in loose, stone-free soil. Thin seedlings for best results.

🌿 Herbs for September

September is excellent for establishing perennial herbs and planting annual varieties:

  • Basil: Plant seedlings in warm, sunny positions. Great companion plant for tomatoes
  • Coriander: Sow seeds directly. Harvest leaves regularly to prevent bolting
  • Parsley: Plant seedlings in rich soil. Both curly and flat-leaf varieties thrive
  • Mint: Plant in containers to control spreading. Perfect for refreshing summer drinks
  • Rosemary: Plant cuttings or seedlings in well-draining soil. Drought-tolerant once established

🌸 Flowers to Brighten Your Garden

Add color and attract pollinators to your garden with these September plantings:

  • Marigolds: Plant seedlings for natural pest control and bright color
  • Zinnias: Sow seeds directly for long-lasting summer blooms
  • Sunflowers: Plant seeds in sunny positions for impressive height and blooms
  • Cosmos: Sow seeds for delicate, daisy-like flowers that attract butterflies
  • Nasturtiums: Plant seeds for edible flowers and trailing beauty

Essential Garden Preparation

Before you start planting, take time to prepare your garden for the growing season ahead:

🌱 Pre-Planting Checklist

🌿

Soil Preparation

Test soil pH and add organic matter like compost or aged manure to improve fertility and structure.

🌤️

Weather Monitoring

Check local weather forecasts and be prepared to protect tender seedlings from unexpected cold snaps.

💧

Irrigation Setup

Ensure your watering system is working properly. Young plants need consistent moisture.

🛡️

Pest Prevention

Install physical barriers like netting for birds and consider companion planting for natural pest control.

Climate Zone Considerations

Australia's diverse climate zones mean September gardening varies significantly across the country:

🌡️ Tropical & Subtropical Zones

In northern Australia, September is still part of the dry season. Focus on drought-tolerant plants and ensure adequate irrigation. Plant heat-loving vegetables like okra, sweet potato, and tropical herbs.

🌡️ Temperate Zones

Most of Australia's population lives in temperate zones where September offers perfect spring conditions. This is prime time for the full range of spring vegetables and flowers.

🌡️ Cool & Alpine Zones

In cooler regions, September may still have frost risks. Wait until the last frost date or use protective measures like cloches and row covers for tender plants.

Succession Planting for Continuous Harvest

Don't plant everything at once! Succession planting ensures you have fresh produce throughout the season:

  • Week 1: Plant fast-growing crops like lettuce and radishes
  • Week 2: Add main crops like tomatoes and capsicums
  • Week 3: Plant another round of fast growers
  • Week 4: Add heat-loving crops as temperatures stabilize

Common September Gardening Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced gardeners make these common September mistakes:

  • Planting Too Early: Don't let spring fever cause you to plant before soil temperatures are ready
  • Overwatering: Young plants need moisture but not soggy soil. Check soil before watering
  • Ignoring Frost Dates: Know your area's last frost date and protect tender plants accordingly
  • Poor Spacing: Give plants room to grow. Crowded plants compete for resources and attract pests
  • Forgetting Mulch: Apply organic mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds

September Garden Maintenance Tasks

Keep your garden thriving with these essential September maintenance tasks:

  • Weeding: Remove weeds before they establish and compete with your new plants
  • Pruning: Prune spring-flowering shrubs after they finish blooming
  • Fertilizing: Apply balanced fertilizer to established plants and new seedlings
  • Pest Monitoring: Check regularly for early signs of pest problems
  • Watering Schedule: Establish consistent watering routines for new plantings

Looking Ahead: October Planning

While you're busy with September plantings, start thinking about October:

  • Harvest Planning: Plan how you'll use and preserve your spring harvests
  • Summer Preparation: Consider shade structures and heat protection for summer
  • Succession Crops: Plan what to plant next as early crops finish
  • Pest Management: Develop strategies for summer pest challenges
